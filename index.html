We all have that one friend (or three...) that somehow managed to find a significant 2020. For the rest of us, there's perfect match. Every year, we ask you to fill out a short survey, and in return, we match you with the people most compatible with you based on your answers. In 2020, 5618 hopeful students signed up to meet their perfect match. Let's take a look at how they responded.

I know it feels like you're the only single person left at Cornell (we're seniors, the feeling only gets worse), but we swear, over a third of Cornell's student body participated in our survey. Here's a breakdown of the demographics of the people who took our survey:

There's hope for people seeking partners of the same sex too. 14.7% of men and 14.6% of women who took perfect match indicated that they would like to be matched with a partner of the same sex.

We also asked participants to give the minimum and maximum ages they would be willing to be matched with. We expected men and women to answer differently, but we were not prepared for just how differently men and women responded. This is a look at how age preferences broke down by gender*.
